Lore

The lore of Site-55 is based on real events, from major incidents to everyday operations. It’s updated regularly, allowing readers to understand the site’s history and see how past events shaped its present.

This page contains the main lore of Site-55, including records of key personnel, operational highlights, and notable events. It serves as a central reference for anyone looking to understand the facility and its history.

File Number:

55-Ω | Top Secret

Operational Status:

Active | Secure

Facility Location:

[REDACTED]



Site-55 was established in 1998, founded by Liam and Vabzie as part of an initiative to expand Foundation operations into isolated and secure environments. Their vision was to create a facility completely detached from the surface world one that could contain, study, and secure anomalies beyond the reach of any government.

Founders:

Liam & Vabzie

Purpose:

Isolated Containment

Founded:

1998



Construction began shortly after approval from The Administrator Ceo, head of the O5 Council, who authorized the creation of an underwater site capable of sustaining full scale containment operations. The project, codenamed Project Abyssline, was classified at the highest level, with all details of its location, staffing, and funding permanently sealed under Directive ██-55.

Project Codename:

Abyssline

Security Level:

Top Secret

Depth:

1,200 meters



Head of Construction Celeste led the facility’s construction and systems engineering, developing the pressure resistant architecture and environmental controls that allow Site-55 to operate under immense oceanic depth. Her work formed the foundation of the site’s resilience, ensuring stability through breaches and full lockdowns alike.

Lead Engineer:

Celeste

Structural Integrity:

99.8%

Over the following years, Ibrahim organized and directed the Faction Coordination Hub, allowing Foundation divisions to operate in unison within Site-55. Network design and encrypted communications were maintained by Flevie, who ensured all data flow remained hidden from surface monitoring systems.
